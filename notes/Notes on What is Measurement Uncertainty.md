This is a short paper from Luca Mari objecting to an implicit change in the definition of measurement uncertainty in the 2023 revisions to GUM-1 from the original GUM. The paper protests against these proposed changes and suggests some less radical modifications.

I thought that this paper was a good example of why a more consistent application of a measurement systems perspective would help Mari's philosophy of measurement. Since a measurement system is a system, it has ("properties" or "attributes"), and some of these properties may be emergent. But to know the properties of a measurement system requires another measurement system, which can lead to a vicious circle. One way out is to assume that there is an error-free way of knowing the actual ("true") value of a measured property. This can somewhat be approximated by "preparation" of calibration standards, so that the property of a substance is "known" prior to transduction via a measuring system. Then the quality of a measurement can be compared to the "true value" through the notion of distance from this ideal system. A related idea of a "gold standard" is hypothesized when preparation is not available.
