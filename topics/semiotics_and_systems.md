## Introduction

Measurement systems combine in a fundamental way the notions of "semiotics" and "systems." Mari's last chapter is thoroughly grounded in thinking of measurement as a semiotic process. Since Peirce's initial goals for semiotics were to develop a general science of representation, then representational measurement theory should be expressible as a semiotic discipline. 

As I discussed in the course outline, systems and semiotics have developed as two leading examples of transdisciplines. But these ideas are also developed within engineering as "signals and systems" as the unified study of analog and digital signal processing, still taught in some EE departments, including CalPoly.
