## Introduction

This article is an extension of the discussion of duality in the entry *metrology_mereology_topology" to the broader context of systems philosophy. As noted there, Bogdanov develops a dual principle between organization and disorganization, with systems considered as in balance between these opposing forces. I'd like to trace this idea though 20th century systems theory/philosophy.

It's probably good to keep in mind the difference between duality as expressed by an adjunction and an adjoint triple that expresses the unity and identity of opposites. This latter notion of duality is what is found in duality between monads and comonads, while the former notion is more closely related to the parts/partition duality.
